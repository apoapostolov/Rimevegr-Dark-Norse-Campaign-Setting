# Y315 Iron Barrow Climax and Aftermath

## Purpose

This file owns the decisive Y315 endgame: alliance hardening, descent
toward the Iron Barrow, confrontation with the Galdr-Forge, the sealing or
mishandling of that power, and the hard-won aftermath that determines what
kind of Rimevegr survives.

Boundary rule:

- 23D owns Y314 reconstruction, fracture, and revelation
- 23E owns Y315 climax, sacrifice, aftermath, and post-victory cost
- later ending design should build from the branch seeds defined here
  rather
than pretending the ending arrives without a price

## 1. Prompt 15B — build the Y315 Iron Barrow climax and post-climax

Y315 must feel like the earned convergence of everything built since Y311.
Nothing about the final year should read like a sudden dungeon raid stapled
onto an otherwise political saga. The expedition only becomes possible
because the previous years created the necessary map, debt web, trauma,
knowledge, and coalition pressure.

The climax therefore has five duties:

- gather the surviving powers into one final workable alignment
- force a descent that tests law, command, loyalty, and nerve together
- make the Galdr-Forge confrontation a choice-rich ritual and military
  crisis,
not a single switch being flipped
- ensure sealing the threat costs lives, identity, leverage, or future
  freedom
- make the aftermath count politically, socially, and personally instead of
ending at the moment the hum stops

### Y315 doctrine

- the final expedition must be competence-built and logistically plausible
- sacrifice should emerge from role and responsibility, not random tragedy
- good outcomes must still feel scarred and expensive
- dark outcomes should come from understandable human failures such as
  greed,
vanity, mistrust, or the will to control dangerous knowledge
- the post-climax year-end must show who inherits legitimacy, who loses it,
  and
what kind of peace has actually been purchased

### Endgame pressure streams

| Stream               | What it looks like in play                                                                                            | Why it matters                                       |
| -------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| alliance pressure    | Sigrun, Bryn, Voss, the Widow, and reluctant auxiliaries all need one another but do not want the same future         | the final coalition is real but unstable             |
| ritual pressure      | Thorne's marks, Dalla's altered state, and the forge-runes create a time-sensitive sealing problem                    | the climax is more than a battlefield problem        |
| battlefield pressure | surface defense, chokepoints, retreat discipline, and casualty management decide whether the descent can even succeed | tactical competence remains central to the ending    |
| aftermath pressure   | artifacts, debt, legitimacy, and survivor trauma determine whether victory actually stabilizes the region             | the ending must change the world, not merely save it |

### Resolution lane seeds

These are not the final outcomes yet, but they define the branch families
that Prompt 23 must later formalize.

| Lane seed                 | Core condition established in Y315                                                   | Direction later                                        |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| hard-won seal             | the forge is sealed and the alliance holds just long enough to survive               | supports a fatalistic good ending                      |
| costly stabilization      | the threat is stopped, but the Widow or council consolidates major control afterward | supports a good ending with heavy political compromise |
| wounded half-victory      | the barrow is contained only partially or at major trust cost                        | supports an unstable, morally bitter ending            |
| human or Veil catastrophe | greed, pride, or failed coordination turns the descent into a deeper breach          | supports one of the dark endings                       |

## 2. Main Y315 endgame objects

### O315-E01 — Council of Necessity

- **Year window:** Y315 spring, days 1-42
- **Class:** endgame-muster object
- **POV:** Voss, Gest, Sigrun-facing witness, or Bryn
- **Canon anchors:** EVT_315_S02_062, EVT_315_S04_064, EVT_315_S06_066,
EVT_315_S07_067, EVT_315_S08_068, EVT_315_S09_069, EVT_315_S12_072

  powers
now know the Iron Barrow is the root problem, and the region must decide
what force, law, and price it will commit to one final answer
- **Decision pressure:** who gets trusted with command, knowledge, and the
  right
to decide what happens if the forge can be sealed only at great human cost?
- **Competent options:** form a narrow mission charter, settle Thorne's
  outside
liabilities, unify logistics, and keep the goal limited to survival and
sealing
- **Desperate options:** bargain away the future to the Widow blindly,
  split
command across egos, or chase prestige before secure preparation is
complete
- **Likely state touches:** unified_command, widow_concessions,
expedition_readiness, thorne_protected_status, council_legitimacy
- **Routes:**
  - **O315-E02** — the coalition marches if the charter holds.
  - **O315-E03** — political concessions reshape what happens inside the
    forge.
  - **Prompt 15A** — the audit should test whether this coalition is
    earned.
- **Mystery-box note:** answers who goes and why, but not what each ally
  means to
take back from the depth if given the chance

form the true center of the threat will take underground
