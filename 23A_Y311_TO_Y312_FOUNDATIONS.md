# Y311 to Y315 Foundations

## Purpose

This file locks the campaign's foundational chronology and causal pressure
from Y311 through the eventual Y315 endgame. It is the first companion
document under the 23-series split plan.

Use it to keep later chapter-objects aligned with the known historical
flow. Scene order may flex for drama, but the underlying causal order
should not drift unless the canon data files themselves change.

Boundary rule: this file owns the foundation and transition logic from Y311
through the early Y313 setup. The detailed Deepholm war-phase build now
belongs in 23B so the split stays clean.

## 1. Chronology doctrine

- Y311 is the buried prequel year: grievances, disappearances, debts, and
  first
omens are planted here.
- Y312 is the positioning year: contracts, the Allthing, faction testing,
  and
early barrow evidence all tighten the knot.
- Y313 is the war year: mobilization, the Deepholm campaign, failed
  neutrality,
and attritional collapse.
- Y314 is the revelation year: reconstruction, re-alignment, and the
  emergence
of the deeper truth behind the dead.
- Y315 is the endgame year: the Iron Barrow opens, the expedition descends,
  and
the region pays the full price of the answer.

If a future plot object contradicts these anchors, the object should be
changed, not the chronology.

### Required use of Y311

Y311 should explain why Y312 begins with tension already in motion. It is
not a quiet backstory year. It is the year in which the trap is built
without most of the cast realizing it.

### Y311 prequel pressure layer

The Y311 material should be revealed in pressure-triggered pieces rather
than as one exposition block. Every return to Y311 must answer a live
present-tense problem and sharpen a later choice.

| ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------- |

### Trigger rule for Y311 flashbacks

Only surface Y311 material when the current scene earns it. Use these
trigger families:

- **money and command triggers** — late wages, ugly contracts, disputed
  orders,
or band arguments should unlock Voss and Gest prequel material
- **kin and captivity triggers** — slave routes, southbound rumors,
  letters, and
fosterage or debt talk should unlock Petra's buried track
- **feud and outlaw triggers** — old warband names, parley, blood-price
  talk,
or settlement justice should unlock Thorne's prequel pressure
- **omen triggers** — wrong frost, silent animals, barrow stench, dreams,
  and
rune-mark pain should unlock the earliest supernatural hints

The rule is simple: no flashback unless it answers the present while
opening a more dangerous future question.

## 3. Y312 — Positioning and first fractures

This is the first fully active year of the campaign and the point where
canon anchors become non-negotiable. Prompt 7 should make Y312 feel like a
year of intelligent testing rather than immediate chaos. The story pressure
rises through contracts, patrols, escort work, assemblies, and supply
stress before it hardens into open campaign logic.

### Y312 design doctrine

- most chapters should begin as practical work, travel, or negotiation
  rather
than open battle
- every paid job should also function as intelligence gathering, alignment
testing, or reputation movement
- no major faction should look foolish in Y312; the danger comes from smart
local decisions that collectively build a worse regional future
- supernatural evidence should remain individually deniable but
  cumulatively
impossible to dismiss

### Y312 positioning map by actor

| Actor or bloc | What they are really doing in Y312                                                  | Public face they present                     | What the band learns too late                                                 |
| ------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Ordovast      | measuring loyalty, tribute capacity, grain, roads, and likely resistance before war | restoring order and collecting what is owed  | he is already acting on campaign assumptions long before he admits war intent |
| Sigrun        | binding the coast through trade, guards, and mutual usefulness                      | keeping peace and commerce alive             | her road policy is also defense preparation and alliance sorting              |
| Pale Widow    | building a healer-intelligence network and acquiring leverage through debt          | charity, medicine, and cultivated neutrality | she is turning private weakness into regional power                           |
| Grave Wardens | tracking barrow anomalies and trying not to trigger panic too early                 | local tomb duty and old ritual obligation    | they are already seeing signs that exceed known burial unrest                 |
| Black Axes    | trying to stay paid, mobile, and not politically owned                              | a competent band for hire                    | every contract is quietly choosing the band's future enemies and patrons      |

### Phase A — uneasy spring positioning

**Must-happen anchors from canon:**

- Day 3: thaw floods strike Frostfjord.
- Day 5: Ordovast summons tribute from Iron Grip settlements.
- Day 8: Sigrun opens the coast road for spring trade.
- Day 10: the Pale Widow places an agent in Kolvik.
- Day 12: Voss secures the Black Axes' spring contract.
- Day 15: the Bone Pack is sighted near Ashmark.
- Day 20: Petra receives the letter from her sister.
- Day 47: Grimholt's gate is fortified further.

**Narrative meaning:**

Spring teaches the audience that every faction is already moving before
open war exists. Ordovast applies pressure openly. Sigrun builds capacity
rather than boasting. The Widow moves through soft power, debt, medicine,
and rumor.

The band's early work should show that routine mercenary labor is never
just routine anymore. Road patrols reveal who can still pay. Escort duty
reveals who expects ambush. Tribute runs reveal which settlements are
quietly afraid and which are already preparing to resist.

**What competent Y312 spring scenes should accomplish:**

- show Voss and Gest treating contracts as both pay and reconnaissance
- let Petra's sister thread enter through private urgency, not melodrama
- establish that Ordovast's riders are measuring obedience, not merely
  taking
coin
- seed the first deniable barrow and weather irregularities in the
  background of
ordinary travel

**Mystery-box effect:**

The reader should not yet know whether the real future threat is political
war, mercenary predation, private vendetta, or barrow unrest. All of them
must feel plausible.

### Phase B — Allthing season and public mask-work

**Must-happen anchors from canon:**

- the Allthing is formally proclaimed and convened at Deepholm
- bands can take security and garrison work tied to the assembly
- public prestige and private hatred both rise
- the peace-shield holds only until a brawl and later killing shatter trust

**Narrative meaning:**

Y312's assembly season is where the power map becomes legible. It is also
where neutrality begins to fail. The Black Axes learn that reputation,
contracts, and personal survival are becoming inseparable.

This phase should feel like political theater performed by people who know
the props may soon be dropped. Sigrun uses the law and market order to show
that the Compact can still coordinate. Ordovast tests how much fear he can
create without triggering a united answer. The Widow gathers more through
overheard needs, private bargains, and sickroom gossip than others gain
from open speeches.

**Allthing-season functions for Prompt 7:**

- make the Black Axes visible enough to matter but not powerful enough to
  relax
- show that public peace-forms are still obeyed because everyone fears the
  cost
of breaking them too early
- tie every feast, escort, hall duty, and private talk to later faction
  memory
- let the first stronger supernatural reports arrive while leaders are busy
pretending politics is still the whole game

**Mystery-box effect:**

Every negotiation should seem to answer one question while raising others:

- why is Ordovast acting as though he is already entitled to submission?
- how much has Sigrun prepared for a siege she claims not to expect?
- how much does the Widow know, and who is paying her to know it?

### Prompt 8 — the Allthing power-struggle web

The Allthing should operate as four things at once:

- a law-stage where public claims are made before witnesses
- a contract market where bands and settlements quietly sort winter
  survival
- an intelligence fair where rumors, wounds, debts, and grain numbers are
traded as carefully as silver
- a pride trap where one insult can undo a week of sober bargaining

#### What each major actor is trying to gain

- **Sigrun** wants to prove the Compact can still coordinate trade, law,
  and
defense without kneeling to Grimholt
- **Ordovast** wants recognition of his borders and tribute logic so later
  war
can be framed as enforcement rather than naked expansion
- **the Pale Widow** wants access, debt, and knowledge rather than
  applause;
if others leave the hall feeling stronger while she leaves knowing more,
she has won
- **the Black Axes** want pay, standing, and freedom of movement without
  being
permanently owned by any one bloc

#### Assembly-season pressure tracks

- **witness pressure** — what is said publicly cannot be fully recalled
  later
- **escort pressure** — who guards whom reveals hidden alignment even
  before
formal oaths are sworn
- **grain and winter pressure** — hall-space, provisioning, and future
  contracts
sit behind nearly every speech
- **rumor pressure** — private information changes value minute by minute
  and is
worth more than bragging steel

#### Prompt 8 implementation rule

A good Allthing chapter should never be only talk. Every argument should
change later access, winter options, hidden hostility, or the band's usable
reputation. If a scene at Deepholm produces no practical shift, it is not
doing enough work.

### Phase C — autumn strain and early winter choices

**Must-happen anchors from canon:**

- barrow trouble around Feldwick and the wider region intensifies
- Dalla predicts an early and unusually hard winter
- winter hall and winter-pay decisions begin sorting bands by loyalty and
  need
- hunger, debt, and shelter questions become immediate rather than
  theoretical

**Narrative meaning:**

This phase converts summer politics into bodily stakes. Every decision
about silver, grain, shelter, and employer now feeds directly into survival
and later alignment.

Autumn should also make clear that Y312 is not merely a prelude year. It is
the season in which small practical decisions become binding. If Voss
accepts a winter garrison, he gains shelter but loses mobility. If he
declines, the band keeps freedom but risks hunger, desertion, and political
irrelevance. Petra's letters grow more desperate. Kell's debt web becomes
harder to hide. Thorne's past begins to walk toward him.

**Prompt 7 autumn priorities:**

- turn grain price spikes and bad harvest news into immediate morale
  pressure
- show bands competing for winter security rather than behaving like free
  heroes
- make the Feldwick barrow opening feel like the first undeniable pattern
  event
- let outlaw and rival-band friction prove how thin legitimacy really is

### Phase D — the first southward crisis

**Must-happen anchors from canon:**

- the Grave Wardens issue a desperate plea over barrow danger
- Ordovast marches south with real siege intent
- Sigrun calls unaligned bands to Deepholm's defense
- midwinter marks the Veil at its thinnest
- Deepholm's gates remain shut and the first siege or standoff begins
- Ordovast eventually retreats, having spent men and stores for too little
  gain

**Narrative meaning:**

The last movement of Y312 should not yet feel like full war-season mastery.
It should feel like the point where every careful local posture breaks down
at once. The law is still spoken, but fewer people believe it can hold. The
band can no longer pretend to work outside the main political weather.

### Prompt 9 — late Y312 winter survival convergence

Prompt 9 should make winter feel like the season where every earlier
bargain is collected in the body's own coin: cold, hunger, wounded pride,
shrinking roads, and ugly dependence.

#### Winter convergence streams

| Pressure stream                | What it looks like in play                                                        | Why it matters structurally                                 |
| ------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| hunger and price shock         | grain doubles, halls hoard, and every meal becomes an argument about tomorrow     | proves that economics route loyalty as strongly as ideology |
| shelter and hall access        | bands scramble for roofs, firewood, and legal permission to winter in place       | removes the illusion of total mercenary freedom             |
| debt and morale fracture       | Kell's lies, Gest's counting, and short pay start poisoning trust inside the band | personal weakness now has campaign-level cost               |
| outlaw and rival-band pressure | starving wolfsheads, route tolls, and opportunistic rivals exploit bad weather    | shows how legitimacy thins when roads fail                  |
| barrow dread                   | split mounds, dead names, bad dreams, and Warden warnings turn fear into pattern  | shifts the tone from rumor to undeniable encroachment       |

#### Winter-arc implementation rule

Every winter chapter should force a trade-off between at least two
necessities:

- food versus mobility
- shelter versus independence
- mercy versus security
- honor versus survival
- public law versus what men will actually do in the dark

If a winter scene does not cost comfort, trust, or freedom, it is too soft
for this phase.

**Bridge into later prompts:**

- Prompt 10 will show how Y313 shifts from warning signs into actual
  campaign
preparation.

### Locked result of Y312

By the end of the year, the whole region understands four things:

1. war is no longer theoretical
2. neutrality is becoming a luxury only the powerful can fake
3. contracts and grain are shaping allegiance as much as oath and rhetoric
4. the supernatural problem is widening during the exact moment human order
   is
being drained by military ambition

### Prompt 7 object build targets

These should guide the next passes and keep the Y312 year legible:

- **O312-P01** — tribute riders reveal who is already under pressure
- **O312-C01** — the band's first real contract establishes pay versus
  honor
- **O312-P04** — the Allthing becomes a law-mask over raw positioning
- **O312-A01** — autumn provisioning and winter-hall choice force alignment
- **O312-W01** — the first Deepholm crisis proves that every earlier choice
  now
has military consequences

## 4. Y313 — Open war and attrition

Y313 should feel like the year in which everything that was still deniable
in Y312 becomes public, costly, and impossible to contain through talk
alone.

### Canon war anchors

- thaw reveals the previous siege dead outside Deepholm
- Ordovast regroups brutally at Grimholt and renews oaths
- Sigrun fortifies the southern pass and levies for real war
- the Pale Widow consolidates Kolvik under debt-backed control
- Voss and Gest count the band's weakness in silver, rations, and injured
  men
- the Allthing is proclaimed under war clouds and collapses in armed
  tension
- Ordovast marches south with eighty men, supply wagons, and siege intent
- the Battle of the Deepholm Gate proves this is a true campaign, not a
  raid
- rationing, failed harvests, desertion, and winter privation hollow out
  all
sides afterward

### Narrative purpose of Y313

This is the competence-and-attrition year. Tactics matter. Supply matters.
Retreat routes matter. Weather matters. The audience should feel that both
great ambition and small daily errors are killing people.

### Prompt 10 — early Y313 strategic mobilization

Early Y313 should show that serious leaders prepare for war before banners
fully unfurl. Nobody competent simply waits for the clash. They count
bodies, inspect roads, move grain, test loyalties, prepare fallback points,
and decide which losses can be survived.

#### Mobilization realities to emphasize

| Pressure line         | Ordovast's method                                                    | Sigrun's method                                                         | What it means for the band                                         |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| manpower              | coercive oath renewal, executions, forced levies, visible punishment | practical levy calls, pass fortification, arming traders and townsmen   | every contract now competes with the war machine for bodies        |
| logistics             | hoarded grain, conscripted labor, concentrated force around Grimholt | distributed stores, road defense, harbor taxation, compact coordination | food and cart access matter as much as blade skill                 |
| intelligence          | scouts at borders, emissaries, fear-based silence                    | road reports, merchant gossip, witness networks                         | Gest and Petra become as valuable as fighters                      |
| morale and legitimacy | rule through dread and inevitability                                 | rule through usefulness and lawful witness                              | neutral bands are pressured to prove what kind of order they serve |

#### Prompt 10 implementation rule

If a scene claims mobilization is happening, it should show one or more of
the following on the page:

- scouts or patrol screens probing roads and passes
- real accounting of grain, carts, wounds, or available fighters
- law, oath, or tax pressure altering local behavior
- deception, misinformation, or concealment about where force will be used

War preparation is not backdrop. It is the story.

### Prompt 11 — the southward march and screening war

Prompt 11 should turn early war into movement, misdirection, and controlled
violence rather than instantly jumping to one giant battle. The key
question is who can march, feed, screen, and reposition better under
stress.

#### Operational truths for the march phase

| Operational need | What competent commanders do                                                | Story consequence                                                     |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| road control     | secure crossings, passes, and choke points before the main body arrives     | smaller actions determine who reaches the battlefield tired or intact |
| screening        | send scouts, outriders, and sacrificial light forces ahead of the core host | uncertainty becomes a weapon and information has body cost            |
| wagon discipline | guard the grain, tools, and spare gear that let an army keep moving         | one burned cart can matter more than a dozen boastful kills           |
| retreat logic    | preserve fallback routes and rally points before fighting starts            | smart leaders plan survival even while attacking                      |

#### Prompt 11 implementation rule

A march scene should always answer at least one of these questions:

- who controls the next road, ford, or ridge?
- which force is moving faster or with better supply protection?
- what does the commander believe is ahead, and how certain is that belief?
- what friction is degrading the plan before the real clash begins?

#### March-phase object build targets

- **O313-W03** — screening clashes and route denial before the main battle
  line
- **O313-W04** — the southward host under wagon, weather, and morale
  pressure
- **O313-W05** — Deepholm approach and outer-defense testing

### Mystery-box effect

Even as the military story sharpens, the campaign should deepen three
linked uncertainties:

- is the war weakening the land, or did someone knowingly choose the war
  because
the land was already weakening?
- who understands the barrow pattern earlier than they admit?
- why do the dead seem to be converging toward the Deepholm axis?

## 5. Y314 — Reconstruction and revelation

Y314 begins like a recovery year and must slowly reveal that recovery is
only partial, conditional, and possibly too late.

### Canon revelation anchors

- Frostfjord reads the names of the dead aloud in spring
- Ordovast claims victory despite obvious strategic failure
- Sigrun rebuilds and expands the Compact on pragmatic terms
- Petra's sister finally arrives, but not without a price coming due
- the barrow fields go unnaturally quiet
- the Bone Pack absorbs the remnants of the Hollow Hall
- Dalla's condition deepens and Thorne discovers the galdr fragment
- the Grave Wardens recruit at unprecedented scale
- later revelations point toward the Galdr-Forge and the truth behind the
  wider
dead movement

### Locked result of Y314

The campaign stops asking whether order can simply be restored and starts
asking what damaged version of order, if any, can survive once the deeper
mechanism is understood.

## 6. Y315 — Endgame and paid-for survival

Y315 must be treated as the culmination year rather than a brief
denouement. The region is no longer merely managing threat; it is living
inside the failure state created by the previous years.

### Canon endgame anchors

- the Iron Barrow opens fully and draugr activity surges across the network
- Dalla is forced into accelerated galdr preparation
- Thorne accepts that his curse is part of the route inward
- a joint expedition of Wardens, Black Axes, and settlement fighters is
  formed
- the descent into the Iron Barrow becomes the campaign's decisive action
- the forge chamber and unbinding determine which endings remain possible
- survival after success still carries scars, grief, and political fallout

### Narrative function of Y315

Y315 resolves the question of whether competence, sacrifice, and limited
trust can still preserve a wounded society after years of bad choices and
supernatural bleeding. It is the year of answer, cost, and inheritance.
