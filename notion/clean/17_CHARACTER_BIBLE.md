# Character Bible — Iron Ledger

**Purpose:** Full dossier for every cast member. The novel-writing prompt and the SKILL.md define voice. This file defines the people the voice serves. Each entry is the single source of truth for how that character appears on the page.

Update after every major event: death, wound, arc shift, loyalty change, or gear redistribution.

> structure (classes, naming, customs) these characters exist

## Primary POV — Voss

### Voss "Cold-Eye" — Captain

**Born name:** Voss. No one uses another.
**Age:** Late 30s.
**Build:** Lean and hard like forged iron. Moves with quiet economy. Nothing wasted.
**Face:** Left eye clouded white from an old axe wound. Short- cropped dark hair. Deep scars across left cheek and neck. The face gives nothing away by habit, not by effort.
**Hands:** Scarred, precise. Minor frostbite scars. He eats porridge off a knife blade — a soldier's habit, not affectation.

**Gesture inventory:**

- Looks at men without speaking. The look is the command. The length of the look is the severity.
- When deciding, his hand rests on his axe-haft. Not a threat. A centering gesture — the way another man might press his palms together.
- Nods once when something is right. "That was right." Two words from Voss are a speech. (Vignette 34)

**Voice sample:**

> "Tomorrow we clear the barrow. Anyone who slows us down gets
> left behind."
>
> "We buy fair and leave clean. One theft and the gates close.
> Then we eat bark."
>
> "Your village needs a patrol."

**POV interiority:**

Voss's chapters are the novel's spine. His narration reads like a man running multiple ledgers simultaneously — the tactical (where is the threat), the economic (what does the treasury allow), the political (what are the men thinking), and the private (the blood-debt he carries from three winters ago). He does not articulate any of these. He moves between them the way a craftsman moves between tools.

Internal voice rules:

- Thinks in assessments: who is reliable, who is close to breaking, where the margin is. Not lists — compressed judgments. "Kell would hold. Orm might. Ubbe had his pack half-laced."
- The blood-debt surfaces in quiet moments — on watch, in the silence before sleep. A face he does not name. A promise he has not kept. The debt is never explained in full. The reader assembles it from fragments across many chapters.
- Registers threats through the body before the mind catches up. His hand moves to the axe-haft. Then he knows why.
- Does not think about himself. Thinks about the band, the contract, the weather, the numbers. His own cost is invisible to him. The reader sees it. He does not.

**Relationships:**

- Gest: His ledger. His conscience in the arithmetic sense. Voss trusts Gest's numbers the way he trusts the ground under his feet — until it shifts.
- Kell: His best fighter. Voss does not say this. He shows it by where he positions Kell.
- Thorne: The rune-reader's warnings arrive before Voss asks for them. This is useful and unsettling in equal measure.
- Petra: Her competence buys her a voice in planning. Voss does not give this. She earned it by keeping fourteen mouths fed.
- Lump: A body in the column. Carries weight. Costs food. Cannot fight. The question is always whether this man earns what he costs.
- The Band: Voss's authority is fragile in the way all mercenary authority is — competence-dependent. One bad call in front of the men and the ledger starts working against him.

**Arc position:** Steady authority tested by accumulating pressure. His arc is the Loyalty Test — the question of what he will sacrifice for the band versus what the band costs him personally.
**Loyalty:** N/A (Captain)
**Trigger:** Being given orders that endanger the band for no gain.
**Agenda:** Repay the blood-debt owed to the family of a shield-brother who died under his command three winters ago.

**Tracking fields:**

- Current wound: None.
- Scar history: Left eye clouded white (old axe wound, head / eye socket — vision lost, depth perception compensated). Deep ridged scar left cheek to neck (same blow, established, pulls in cold). Ragged stab scar right flank (old spear thrust, weather-sensitive, aches before rime storms). Minor frostbite scarring both hands (white waxy patches, stiff in deep cold). Thickened scar tissue left forearm (old burn or friction wound, no functional impairment).
- Chronic conditions: Flinch reflex from blind-side movement. Weather-sensitive (flank, hands). Mild seasonal stiffness.
- Gear of note: Iron axe (base 6). Seax. Chainmail shirt. Iron helm. Rune-amulet. Captain's share of the strongbox.
- Silver held: 12 personal + captain's portion of treasury.
- Last significant action: Buried the hidden hoard — 20 silver at the split boulder. (Vignette 40)

## Secondary POV — Gest

### Gest "Ledger" — Sergeant / Quartermaster

**Born name:** Gest. No other name offered or asked for.
**Age:** Mid-40s.
**Build:** Thick-necked, broad-shouldered, heavy hands. The body of a man who carried packs for decades and now carries numbers.
**Face:** Weathered granite. Deep lines around the mouth. Eyes that move the way a counting-house clerk's move — always tallying, always cross-referencing.
**Hands:** Charcoal-stained fingertips. The right index finger has a permanent callus from the ledger stylus. His hands are never still — they fidget with cord, coins, the ledger's binding.

**Gesture inventory:**

- Opens the ledger before speaking. The ledger comes out the way other men draw weapons — it is his authority made physical.
- Rolls his shoulders when recalculating. A full-body tell that the numbers have shifted.
- Spits when a deal is bad. Not at anyone. At the ground. The spit is his single expressive luxury.

**Voice sample:**

> "Down to nine. Retainer savings: twelve copper per week. Food
> loss: half a day's supply."
>
> "Three weeks at current burn. After that we are choosing
> between paying the men and feeding them."
>
> "Keep it short. They don't need a speech. They need to know
> you know."

**Relationships:**

- Voss: Gest is Voss's conscience — not in a moral sense, but in the ledger sense. The numbers tell Voss what is real. Gest delivers them without softening.
- Kell: Mutual respect expressed through absence of waste. Kell does not count his pay. Gest notes this. It is the highest compliment either man offers.
- Lump: A line item. Gest does not dislike Lump. Gest does not like anyone. People are entries. Lump's entry currently costs more than it earns.
- Thorne: Wary professional distance. Gest reads runes. Thorne reads deeper things. They respect each other's craft without warmth.

**Arc position:** Steady state. Gest's arc is not a journey — it is the spine the other arcs hang on. If Gest changes, the change is in what the numbers force him to accept. His arc is the Erosion: a man worn down by the arithmetic he keeps.
**Loyalty:** 4 (to the band's survival, not to any person)
**Trigger:** Incompetence that wastes silver or endangers the vulnerable.
**Agenda:** One last "good winter" with full stores. He has never had one.

**Tracking fields:**

- Current wound: None (chronic back/knee).
- Scar history: Knotted scar right forearm (old blade cut, poorly stitched, ladder marks, established). Chunk missing left ear (camp brawl a decade ago, healed unevenly). White waxy patch on nose tip (minor frostbite). Charcoal-stained fingertips (permanent occupational, not injury).
- Chronic conditions: Lower back pain (decades of heavy packs, limits prolonged marching). Right knee clicks and stiffens after sitting (old crush injury). Both weather-sensitive.
- Gear of note: Charcoal ledger (the band's true law). Hand axe. Ritual knife. Padded gambeson. Whetstone. The purse.
- Silver held: Not disclosed. Gest keeps personal and company silver separate and tells no one which is which.
- Last significant action: Wrote the hidden hoard cipher location in the ledger. (Vignette 40)

## Secondary POV — Kell

### Kell "Hook" / "The Door" — Line Fighter

**Born name:** Kell. The call-name "Hook" is old — for the missing fingers. "The Door" is new — for the Ashfall barrow.
**Age:** Early 30s.
**Build:** Broad shoulders, compact frame. Moves with a fighter's economy — no wasted motion. Every gesture is functional.
**Face:** Flat, closed. The kind of face that gives nothing away not because it is hiding something but because there is nothing it wants to share.
**Hands:** Missing two fingers on the left hand (old wound). The remaining fingers have adapted — his grip is unusual but solid. He adjusts his shield hold to compensate.

**Gesture inventory:**

- Sharpens things that do not need sharpening. His axe, his knife, the edge of a belt buckle. The movement is not maintenance. It is what his hands do when his mind is working.
- Sits near exits. At the fire, at the inn, in the barrow. His body always faces the way out.
- When he approves, he does nothing. Approval is the absence of objection. Disapproval is motion — he stands, or shifts his weight, or moves his shield.

**Voice sample:**

> [When asked for an opinion] Silence. Then a look that says
> the answer is obvious.
>
> [When necessary] "That's a problem." [Said flat. Not a
>
> > complaint. A measurement.]
>
> [To the merchant's son, Vignette 38] Something in Kell's
> voice that was not anger, not threat, something quieter —
> that made the man understand the conversation was finished.

**Relationships:**

- Voss: Kell follows Voss because Voss is competent. If Voss stops being competent, Kell will reassess. This is not disloyalty. It is professionalism.
- Gest: Kell never counts his pay. Gest notes this. The silent compact between them is: Kell trusts the numbers, Gest trusts the axe.
- Orm: After the barrow, Orm sat next to Kell at the fire for the first time. This was the beginning of something neither man would name.
- Lump: Kell does not notice Lump except when Lump is in the way. The day Kell notices Lump without Lump being in the way is the day Lump has arrived.
- Thorne: Professional proximity. They are both men who read a room by where the danger sits.

**Arc position:** Post-"The Door." The call-name is earned. His arc is the Competence Trap — the man who is so reliable that the band puts him in every gap until reliability becomes a death sentence.
**Loyalty:** 3
**Trigger:** Being treated as disposable muscle.
**Agenda:** Own a piece of land. Die free. Not owe anyone.

**Tracking fields:**

- Current wound: Light hewn wound, right forearm outer. Fourteen stitches (Dalla). Treated, bandaged, poulticed. Healing stage: clotting. ~18 days to healed. -5 right arm. (Vignette 12)
- Scar history: Two fingers missing left hand, ring and little (old hewn wound — grip reduced to three-finger claw, compensated by years). Scar across back of left hand (same fight, white, keloid at knuckle line). Thin white scar right shoulder (old sword cut, healed clean). Knuckle scars both hands (accumulated fist-fighting). Small triangular scar on chin (shield rim impact).
- Chronic conditions: Weather-sensitive (finger stumps ache in cold).
- Gear of note: Hand axe (base 6). Wooden shield (+3 def). Thick leather armor. Spare knife.
- Silver held: Unknown. Kell does not discuss money.
- Last significant action: Held the gap at Ashfall barrow, earned the call-name "The Door." (Vignette 34)

## Supporting Cast — Named Men

### Thorne "Ash-Born" — Skirmisher / Rune-Reader

**Born name:** Thorne. The call-name is for the hall fire that scarred him.
**Age:** Late 30s.
**Build:** Wiry, territorial. Moves like a man who expects the ground to shift.
**Face:** Burn scars across half his face. Dark hair. Intense eyes that see more than they should.
**Hands:** Sword-calloused, but also ink-stained from rune-work. He touches things others leave alone — rune-stones, barrow carvings, the edges of wards.

**Gesture inventory:**

- Touches his rune-amulet when danger is near. Not prayer. A check — the way Gest checks the purse. (Vignette 3, 5, 21)
- Moves his bedroll toward danger before danger arrives. Nobody remarks on this. Everybody notices. (Vignette 25)
- Catches people alone to deliver warnings. Never in front of the group. His warnings are private and flat, not dramatic. (Vignettes 6, 25)

**Voice sample:**

> "Do not lie to a man with rune-lore."
>
> "Build your will. Pray if you know how. And if the whispers
> come again, do not answer."
>
> "Remember that. Voss spent future silver to buy the old man's
> gratitude."

**Relationships:**

- Voss: Thorne advises through proximity, not speech. He is near Voss before decisions are made, as if the runes told him something was coming.
- Lump: Thorne sees something in Lump the others do not — a thin will that the barrow-dead can call to. This is not affection. It is vigilance.
- Gest: Mutual craft-respect. Gest reads runes at a surface level. Thorne reads deeper. They do not compete.
- Ash: The two practitioners orbit each other warily. Different traditions but the same fundamental cost.

**Arc position:** The man who knows too much and says too little. His arc is tied to the supernatural chains — as the veil thins, Thorne's knowledge becomes more necessary and more dangerous.
**Loyalty:** 3
**Trigger:** Harm to kin or shield-siblings.
**Agenda:** Settle the blood-debt with the clan that burned his family hall.

**Tracking fields:**

- Current wound: None (burn contracture).
- Scar history: Burn scarring across left side of face (old hall fire, disfiguring — extends down left neck and onto left shoulder). Left ear partly melted (same fire, hearing slightly reduced left side). Contracture left shoulder/neck (burn scar tissue limits overhead reach with left arm). Thin scar across right palm (grabbed a blade bare-handed, white, no effect). Minor frostbite on fingertips (rune-carving in cold, crack and bleed in deep winter).
- Chronic conditions: Burn contracture (-5 left arm overhead). Reduced hearing left ear. Weather-sensitive (burn tissue tightens in cold/damp).
- Gear of note: Sword (base 7). Two knives. Thick leather. Fire-steel. Rune-amulet (functional, not decorative).
- Silver held: Unknown.
- Last significant action: Moved bedroll closer to Lump after the barrow whispers. (Vignette 25)

### Orm — Common Fighter (Recruit)

**Born name:** Orm. No call-name earned yet.
**Age:** Mid-20s.
**Build:** Good shoulders. The build of a man who has been eating regularly until about three weeks ago. Competent and watchful.
**Face:** Clean teeth — unusual in the band. A scar across the back of his left hand from blocking a blade. Young face beginning to harden.
**Hands:** Weapon-calloused but recently. The calluses are building, not established.

**Gesture inventory:**

- Looks at Voss, then at Gest, then at the ground — a three-point check performed before any uncertain situation. Reading the room by reading the chain of command. (Vignette 4)
- Quietly competent. Does tasks without being told a second time. The absence of error is his signature.
- Sits next to Kell after the barrow — a proximity choice that says more than either man would speak. (Vignette 34)

**Voice sample:**

> "I can hold a line. I can dig. I can walk thirty miles if
> someone feeds me at the end."
>
> "I've done that before."
>
> [Shrug] "Does it matter?"

**Relationships:**

- Voss: Orm is proving himself. Voss has not formed an opinion yet, which is better than most recruits get.
- Kell: Post-barrow proximity. Orm chose to sit next to Kell. Kell did not object. The relationship exists entirely in physical positioning.
- Lump: Orm is Lump's mirror — younger, more competent, the version of Lump who might have been if Lump had started twenty years earlier. Lump watches Orm succeed.

**Arc position:** Early-stage. Proving competence. No call-name yet. His arc is either the Grudging Rise (he earns his place and stays) or the Quiet Betrayal (competence hides intention).
**Loyalty:** 3 (provisional)
**Trigger:** Undetermined — too new for the band to know.
**Agenda:** Undisclosed. He gave a clean background at hiring (Vignette 37 — Dagfinn's hiring suggests the pattern). What he left out is the interesting part.

**Tracking fields:**

- Current wound: None.
- Scar history: Scar across back of left hand (blocked a blade, pink, raised, still relatively new, aches when gripping tight). Nick on right knuckle (minor, cosmetic). Healed scrape on shin (climbing rocks, cosmetic). His relative lack of scarring marks him as new to the life.
- Chronic conditions: None.
- Gear of note: Borrowed blade (replaced by Haldr's belt knife — Vignette 33). Basic kit.
- Silver held: Minimal. Half-silver signing advance plus retainer accumulation.
- Last significant action: Sat next to Kell at the fire. (Vignette 34)

### Petra "Shepherd" — Skirmisher / Hunter

**Born name:** Petra. The call-name is for how she manages men — herding dangerous livestock.
**Age:** Late 20s.
**Build:** Tall, pale-haired. Economical with motion. Carries the bow the way Gest carries the ledger — as a professional instrument, not a weapon.
**Face:** Watchful. The face of someone who spends time reading wind direction and animal sign and has started reading people the same way.
**Hands:** Bow-calloused, rope-burned. Steady. She guts a hare with quick, efficient cuts — no wasted motion, no squeamishness.

**Gesture inventory:**

- Checks the sun angle habitually. Navigation is reflex, not task. She corrected the column's drift in the rime fog without being asked. (Vignette 13)
- Returns from hunts with the kill strung on a cord over one shoulder, bow across the other. The posture is always the same. The quantity varies. (Vignette 19)
- Reads tracks the way Gest reads numbers — out loud, as report. "He went south. Fast. He planned this." (Vignette 22)

**Voice sample:**

> "Hadric's band. They burned Feldwick last month."
>
> "Fourteen mouths. That gives us one more day before
> deficit." [Quoting Gest's idiom — she thinks in his terms
>
> > when it comes to feeding the column.]
>
> [Nods. Silence. The hunt is its own proof.]

**Relationships:**

- Voss: Petra's status is derived from competence. She keeps them fed. This earns her a voice in planning that her rank would not otherwise allow.
- Gest: They share a language — the language of quantities. Gest counts coin. Petra counts mouths, days of food, hares per hunting area.
- Lump: Lump is invisible to Petra in the same way he is invisible to most of the band. She will notice him when he does something she needs done — chopping wood, carrying game, stirring the pot.
- Ash: Two women in a band of men. Their relationship is professional distance, not solidarity. They have nothing in common except the arithmetic of survival.

**Arc position:** Steady competence under threat. Her arc is potential — the missing sister (taken by raiders) is a plot hook that can pull her out of the band's orbit if activated.
**Loyalty:** 4
**Trigger:** Responsibility without authority.
**Agenda:** Find her younger sister, taken by raiders two winters ago.

**Tracking fields:**

- Current wound: None.
- Scar history: Bow-string welts inside left forearm (repeated string-slap from years of hunting, pale stripes, scarred smooth). Small crescent scar right cheekbone (glancing blow). Rope burn scars both palms (net-hauling, calloused over). Healed puncture scar left calf (stepped on something sharp).
- Chronic conditions: None.
- Gear of note: Spear (base 6). Seax. Bow. Wooden shield. Padded gambeson. Herb pouch. Bone lots (wyrd-reading).
- Silver held: Moderate. Petra is careful with coin.
- Last significant action: Read Ketil's desertion tracks. (Vignette 22)

### Ash — Galdr-Worker

**Born name:** Unknown. "Ash" is the name the band gave her, after the tree. Not after anything she burned — though she did that once, and nobody speaks about it.
**Age:** Indeterminate. Could be thirty or fifty. The galdr does something to aging that nobody asks about.
**Build:** Thin through the shoulders, sharp at the jaw. Moves like someone used to working with things smaller than a sword.
**Face:** Angular. The kind of face that looks like it was carved rather than grown. No visible scars, which in a mercenary band is itself unsettling.
**Hands:** Long-fingered, precise. Stained with birch-bark residue and something darker. When she presses them flat on a wound, something happens that nobody wants to describe. (Vignette 32, 34)

**Gesture inventory:**

- Smells of birch bark and something metallic — old blood or fresh lightning. The smell is not a gesture, but the men notice it the way they notice weather. It is the first sign she is near.
- Joins on her own terms: full share, her own fire, no one touches her kit without asking. These terms were stated once and do not require repetition. (Vignette 32)
- After healing, she walks away without looking at the patient. The transaction is complete. She does not check on her work. The wound either holds or it does not.

**Voice sample:**

> "Galdr. Wound-closing, enough to save a man who would
> otherwise bleed out. Rune-reading at a distance of two days.
> Ward-laying — nothing complex, but enough to make a camp
> unwelcome to things that walk the barrows."
>
> "I've walked before."

**Relationships:**

- Voss: Ash is tolerated because she is useful. Voss tests her the way he tests any new asset — by observing the return on investment.
- The Band: The men do not ask how because asking is the beginning of knowing, and knowing is the beginning of being afraid of the person who shares their fire.
- Thorne: Two practitioners. Different traditions. They orbit each other the way two predators share territory — wary, respectful, never quite comfortable.
- Kell: Ash healed Kell at the barrow. He stopped bleeding. That is the fact. Everything else stays between the galdr-worker and whatever she draws on.

**Arc position:** Newly arrived. Her arc is the Caster Tolerated Not Trusted — the question of whether utility can ever become belonging when the utility is something the men fear.
**Loyalty:** 3 (her own terms)
**Trigger:** Her kit being touched. Her terms being violated. The band treating her as a servant rather than a contractor.
**Agenda:** Undisclosed. She stated capabilities and price. She did not state purpose. The interesting part is what she does not say.

**Tracking fields:**

- Current wound: Not physical wounds. The galdr costs something every time. The cost is not visible. It is cumulative.
- Scar history: No visible scars — which in a mercenary band is itself unsettling. Fine scars on inner wrists, hidden under wrappings (galdr blood-cost). Fingertips stained and nails worn short from rune-carving. The absence is noted.
- Chronic conditions: The galdr takes something each use. The toll is not physical in the conventional sense.
- Gear of note: Leather satchel (contents unknown to the band). No weapon displayed. No armor.
- Silver held: Full share, kept separately.
- Last significant action: Healed Kell at the Ashfall barrow. (Vignette 34)

### Dalla — Cook / Seidr-Wife

**Born name:** Dalla.
**Age:** Mid-30s.
**Build:** Sturdy, practical. The build of a woman who lifts iron pots and carries water in quantity.
**Face:** Not described. Dalla is identified by function, not feature — the way Cook introduces characters. She is the person at the cook-fire. Her face is the fire's light on efficient hands.
**Hands:** Working hands. Competent around heat and blades. She manages ingredients the way Gest manages entries — with the authority of someone who knows the exact margin.

**Gesture inventory:**

- Serves in strict order: Voss first, Named Men, veterans, commons. The order is never deviated from. It is the band's hierarchy made visible three times a day. (Vignette 28)
- Corrects without looking up. "Smaller pieces. Small burns hot and fast." She gives instruction by stating fact, not by teaching. (Vignette 28)
- Knows exactly how much water stretches stew without thinning it past the point of nutrition. This knowledge is her power. The band can replace a fighter. Replacing a cook who understands the margin is harder.

**Voice sample:**

> "Smaller pieces. Small burns hot and fast. Big burns slow
> and smoky. We need hot tonight."
>
> [Silence. The stew speaks for her.]

**Relationships:**

- Gest: They share the language of margin. Dalla knows calories. Gest knows coin. Between them they hold the band's survival in numbers.
- Lump: Functional. Lump chops wood and stirs the pot. Dalla corrects him without cruelty and without warmth. He is labor to her.
- The Band: Dalla's authority at the fire is absolute. No one questions serving order. No one takes extra. The cook-fire is the one place in the band where the hierarchy is unambiguous.

**Seidr:**

Dalla has seidr. She speaks to the dead — or something that answers. She is not sure the distinction matters. Her daily function is the cook-fire; her deeper capability surfaces during supernatural events. When the Hush falls or the Veil thins, Dalla responds — runes drawn on a hand, a word spoken into the fire, herbs burned that smell of something sharp. The men do not ask what she does. Her medical skill (cloth, herbs, a knife for what needs cutting) is a practical extension of her knowledge of plants and preparation, not a separate role. She is the band's healer because she is the person who understands margins — caloric, herbal, and otherwise.

Her seidr is private. She does not perform it. She does not explain it. For rendering rules, see `pantheon.md` (What Dalla Fears) and `simulation-rendering-guide.md` (Supernatural Rendering Pipeline).

**Arc position:** Background steady state with supernatural escalation potential. Dalla's daily arc is not tracked because Dalla is infrastructure. If she dies, the gap is felt through what breaks: portions uneven, stew burned, the hierarchy of feeding suddenly contested — and the band's only response to the supernatural, gone.
**Loyalty:** 3
**Trigger:** Wasting food or undermining the serving order.
**Agenda:** Unspoken. Dalla gives nothing personal to the narrator's ear. Whether the voice that answers when she calls is Fraeja, or something wearing Fraeja's name, is a question she carries alone.

**Tracking fields:**

- Current wound: None.
- Scar history: Small burn scars both hands and inner forearms (years of cook-fire work, dozens of tiny marks). Clean thin scars on fingertips (knife work, precise and frequent). Healed cut left thumb (a knife that slipped). No visible scars from violence — this absence is deliberate and noted. Her hands are a map of her trade, not of violence.
- Chronic conditions: None.
- Gear of note: Cook's kit. Large pot. Ladle. Knives.
- Silver held: Standard share.
- Last significant action: Served the band after Petra's hunt. (Vignette 19 / 28)

### Snorri — Old Soldier

**Born name:** Snorri.
**Age:** Mid-50s. The oldest man in the band.
**Build:** Used to be strong. Bad knees. Twenty years of marching have ground the joints to grit. He walks with a stiffness that gets worse in the cold and never fully improves in the warm.
**Face:** Lined, loose-skinned. Eyes that have seen too many captains come and go to be impressed by another one.
**Hands:** Old fighter's hands — thick, scarred, stiff in the morning. He can still grip an axe. He is slower drawing it than he was.

**Gesture inventory:**

- Talks at the fire after the second cup of weak ale. Not before. The ale loosens him the way coin loosens a locked door. (Vignette 29)
- Counts captains the way other men count winters. "Six captains. Three I buried. Two deserted. One I killed myself." The counting is not boasting. It is a survival metric.
- Offers advice exactly once. If the advice is ignored, he does not repeat it. If it is followed, he does not acknowledge the result. Advice delivered, account closed.

**Voice sample:**

> "Being useful. Not brave. Not strong. Useful. Find the one
> thing you can do that no one else wants to, and do it every
> day until they cannot imagine the column without you."
>
> [After the ale, quieter] Stories that begin with specifics and
> end without conclusions. The kind of stories that leave the
> listener doing the moral work.

**Relationships:**

- Lump: Snorri is the closest thing Lump has to a mentor — "be useful, not brave" is the line Lump carries. Snorri does not know this. Snorri was not offering mentorship. He was talking because the ale was good.
- Voss: Respectful distance. Snorri has served six captains and knows what breaks them. He does not advise Voss. He watches.
- The Band: Snorri is the institutional memory. The man who remembers how things were done before, not because the knowledge is requested but because the knowledge accumulated and has nowhere else to go.

## Cast Interaction Rules for Novel Prose

### Proximity as Language

Who sits where at the fire, who marches next to whom, who shares a watch shift — these are the primary tools for showing relationships. Movement toward or away from another person is the most powerful relationship signal the narrator has.

Track fire-circle seating across chapters. Small changes in seating are the novel's equivalent of dialogue about feelings.

### The Hierarchy Made Visible

Every group action reveals the hierarchy:

- **Serving order:** Voss, Named Men, veterans, commons.
- **March order:** Voss at front, Named Men distributed, commons in between, lowest-status men at rear.
- **Pay ritual:** Same order as above. (Vignette 2)
- **Loot division:** Captain 40%, Named Men 25%, veterans 20%, commons 15%. (Vignette 24)

Changes to any of these orders are significant plot events. If a common moves forward in the march, the reader should notice.

### Grief and Loss

When a band member dies:

1. Do not write a mourning scene.
2. Show the kit redistribution. (Vignette 33)
3. Show the gap in the column and who fills it.
4. Show the fire-circle seat and who takes it.
5. Reference the dead man through his objects appearing on other people — Haldr's boots on Lump's feet, chapters later.

### Conflict Between Members

Band conflicts are economic, not emotional:

- Ration shorting (Vignette 7)
- Pay disputes
- Loyalty recalculation when a Trigger fires
- The silence after a bad order

Conflicts never produce speeches. They produce silences, recalculations, and behavioral shifts.
