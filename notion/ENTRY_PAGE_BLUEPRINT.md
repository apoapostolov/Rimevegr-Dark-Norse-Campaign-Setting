# Notion entry page blueprint for Iron Ledger

## Purpose

This page should feel like the front door to the world, not just a file index.
It must create mood in under a minute, explain the setting cleanly, and give a
reader several intuitive ways to enter the material.

This blueprint follows current Notion best practices and the workflow style
used in the official Notion Claude skill material from the Notion cookbook:

- lead with a strong summary and clear structure
- use progressive disclosure instead of a wall of text
- keep navigation visual and scannable
- use columns for short, high-signal blocks only
- use page links and callouts to create guided entry points

---

## Recommended root page setup

- Page title: Rimevegr
- Page icon: 🪓
- Cover image direction: winter fjord, dim longhouse fire, ravens, or a frozen
  road under iron-grey sky
- Full width: enabled
- Small text: disabled
- Table of contents block: do not place one on the front page; reserve TOC for chapter documents and oversized chapter index pages
- Current live snapshot order: page title → hero callout → one summary paragraph → divider → identity columns → navigation hub → guided paths
- Keep the opening lean; do not reintroduce the extra promise callout unless explicitly requested

## Hero opening sequence

### 1. Opening callout

Use a dark, atmospheric opening callout with the icon 🐺 or 🌫️.

Suggested lead text:

> Voss counted heads at the ford and came up one short. The river had not taken
> anyone. The arithmetic was the same as always: feed the ones who stayed, pay
> the ones who fought, bury the ones who fell, and forget the ones who walked
> off in the dark.

This is the right opening because it immediately communicates all of the core
promises of the setting:

- mercenary survival rather than heroic fantasy
- cold realism and quiet dread
- economic pressure as a story engine
- the human cost of every decision

### 2. World summary block

Place a short summary paragraph directly below the vignette.

Suggested summary:

Iron Ledger is a low-magic Norse frontier setting trapped in long cold twilight
after the gods fell silent. Small hard settlements cling to trade, oath, and
firelight while hired bands sell protection, violence, and endurance for silver.
The dead sometimes stir, runes sometimes whisper back, and every kindness has a
cost entered somewhere in the ledger.

### 3. Reader promise callout

Use a second callout with the icon 📜.

Suggested one-line promise:

A world of frost, debt, contract steel, and deniable supernatural dread — built
for fiction, roleplay, and long-form simulation.

## First major section — two-column identity frame

Create two side-by-side callout blocks under the first divider.

### Left column

Heading: What this setting is
Icon: ✅

Content:

- **Mercenary-survival Norse world.** Contracts, hunger, weather, and kin pressure shape nearly every decision, from shelter choices to who gets paid first.
- **Low magic with high consequence.** The supernatural exists, but every use leaves behind bodily, social, or spiritual cost that does not vanish cleanly.
- **Driven by hunger, weather, debt, and oath-keeping.** Logistics, payment, and promises matter as much as violence because one broken obligation can sink a season.
- **Horror through pressure, omen, silence, and cost.** Dread builds gradually through rumor, memory, and accumulated strain instead of exploding into spectacle.
- **Grounded world for novels, campaigns, and simulation play.** The material supports fiction, play, and systems work without losing the human scale.

### Right column

Heading: What this setting is not
Icon: 🚫

Content:

- **Not heroic power fantasy.** People survive by tradeoffs, bargaining, and endurance, not by destiny or exemption from consequence.
- **Not a raid-for-plunder Viking caricature.** Daily life, labor, kin strain, trade, and law matter more than swagger or costume myth.
- **Not high fantasy with chosen ones or dark lords.** The scale stays local, human, uncertain, and resistant to prophecy-driven grandiosity.
- **Not magic as convenience or spectacle.** Wonder appears only when it deepens cost, dread, or moral pressure.
- **Not cruelty for style alone.** Violence carries social, material, and emotional consequences that continue after the scene ends.

Best-practice note:

This section should stay concise. Notion columns work best when the content is
short, bold, and easy to scan. Long prose should stay in full-width sections.

## Second major section — three-column entry hub

Use three columns made of callout cards. Each card should contain short linked
entry points rather than dense explanations.

### Column 1 — World and atmosphere

Heading: Rimevegr
Icon: 🌍

Links to include:

- 🧭 Setting Bible — the canon overview
- 🗺️ Geography and Map — land, travel, and regions
- 🍞 Culture and Customs — daily life and social norms
- 🙏 Religion of the Rimevegr — the silent gods and their rites
- ✨ Magic of Rimevegr — rune-work, seiðr, and dread
- 🌨️ Weather, Seasons, and Hazards — climate pressure
- 📆 Calendar and Hidden Events — the temporal frame
- ⚰️ Barrows of Rimevegr — ancient dead and burial fear

### Column 2 — People, settlements, and conflict

Heading: The Living World
Icon: 🛡️

Links to include:

- 🏘️ Villages and Settlements — the places people still endure
- 👑 Political Villages and Unions — power and rivalry
- ⚔️ Rival Bands and Factions — armed pressure from outside
- 🐺 Wolfsheads of Rimevegr — outlaw threat core
- 🪙 Mercenary Lifestyle — the band-for-hire reality
- 👥 Named Men and Archetypes — recurring human patterns
- 🧍 Character Bible — major cast and relationships

### Column 3 — Story, systems, and use cases

Heading: Play or Write
Icon: 📚

Links to include:

- 🧾 Master Index — the full project map
- 🎲 Game Content Bible — content tables and support data
- ⚙️ Simulation Rules — the engine under the world
- 📊 Band Simulation — current runtime state
- 🛡️ Member Statblocks — individual mechanical profiles
- 🔥 Campaign Arcs and Plot Seeds — long-form story entry
- 🖋️ Vignettes and Scenes — tone, voice, and opening scenes

Best-practice note:

Columns in Notion collapse on smaller screens. Each column therefore needs a
clear heading and self-contained bullet list so the stacked mobile version still
reads naturally.

## Recommended guided entry paths

After the three-column hub, add a section named Start here if you want...

- 📖 to understand the world fast: Setting Bible → Geography and Map → Culture and Customs → Mercenary Lifestyle
- 🧭 to run a campaign: Setting Bible → Villages and Settlements → Rival Bands and Factions → Simulation Rules → Campaign Arcs and Plot Seeds
- ✍️ to write fiction: Vignettes and Scenes → Character Bible → Mercenary Lifestyle → Campaign Arcs and Plot Seeds
- 👻 to lean into the dread: Religion of the Rimevegr → Magic of Rimevegr → Barrows of Rimevegr → Weather, Seasons, and Hazards
- 🧠 to explore the full system: Master Index → Game Content Bible → Simulation Rules → Band Simulation → Member Statblocks

This gives readers multiple valid entry points without forcing a single path.

## Root page composition rules for the uploader

When the pipeline starts creating the actual Notion root page, it should follow
this order:

1. hero vignette callout
2. world summary paragraph
3. divider
4. two-column identity section
5. divider
6. three-column entry hub
7. divider
8. guided entry paths section
9. divider
10. optional project notes or latest update callout

The root page should not try to contain all chapter text. Its job is to invite,
orient, and route.

## Final quality bar

A reader opening the root page should understand, within 30 seconds:

- what kind of world this is
- what tone to expect
- where to click first depending on their goal
- why this setting feels distinct from generic Viking fantasy
