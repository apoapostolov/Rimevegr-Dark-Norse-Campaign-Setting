# Barrow Atlas of the Rimevegr

<!-- notion-export:toc -->


This atlas documents every known barrow, cairn, and burial site across the Rimevegr — compiled from Grave Warden records, seidr practitioner accounts, and the hard-won testimony of mercenary bands who went inside and came back out.

Not all of them came back whole.

---

## Frostfjord Region

### Whispering Barrow (BAR_001)

Four hours south of Frostfjord Hollow, up a ridge trail that turns to loose scree in the last half-mile. The entrance faces west — a stone lintel carved with faded runes, flanked by a standing stone that hums after dark. The sound is low, felt in the chest more than heard in the ears. Locals call it the whispering stone. Inside, the air smells of cold earth and old iron. Jarl Hrothgar has posted a contract to clear it. The dead have been walking since spring of Y312.

### Draugr-Jarl's Crypt (BAR_002)

Not south of Frostfjord — beneath it. The settlement was built on top of this barrow without knowing. The cellar of Hrothgar's mead hall grows colder each winter because something five hundred years old lies pinned to a stone slab with corroding iron stakes directly below. Three generations of village elders maintained the binding rite in secret. The last one died before teaching his heir. The stakes are failing. The chamber below contains upwards of thirty dead, and the Draugr-Jarl's cold kills anything within five metres. Nobody in Frostfjord understands why the cellar freezes. The barrow has a sealed inner vault whose contents are unknown and, by all accounts, terrible.

### Skaldhaven Cairn (BAR_003)

Three hours along the cliff path north of Skaldhaven. A fishermen's cairn — common folk buried with common things. Nets, hooks, a jug of mead. Something broke the cairn open from the inside two winters ago. Three restless dead wander the immediate area. The cliff face is unstable and the approach narrows to a ledge barely wide enough for one man with a shield. Corpse lights drift near the entrance on foggy nights. Skaldhaven pays silver for rune-stone rubbings and relics from any barrow in the region, making this one a starter job for a small band with more courage than sense.

### Bone Road Mound (BAR_004)

Twelve hours into the deep forest along the Bone Road — the bypass route that skirts Ashen Reach through wolf territory. The mound sits at the midpoint, exactly where a band would camp for the night. No visible entrance. Unbroken turf over worked stone. Travellers who sleep near it report voices — low, conversational, as if people were talking just beneath the surface. No saga records who is buried here. The mound has never been opened. Given its position on the only bypass route, whoever sealed it chose the location to be found. Or avoided.

## Grimholt Region

### Iron Barrow (BAR_005)

Five hours south of Grimholt, near the iron mine that feeds the settlement's economy. The barrow opened in late autumn and the miners stopped working the same day. Fourteen or more draugr occupy the upper levels, moving in patrol patterns that suggest a military burial — these were soldiers, and death has not erased the discipline. The Hollow Hall mercenary band camps at the barrow's mouth, apparently unbothered. Whether they have cleared the entrance or reached some arrangement with what is inside remains unclear. Warchief Ordovast has posted a contract to seal it. Rusted weapons protrude from the entrance rubble — iron-age, pre-settlement.

### Ordovast's Shame (BAR_006)

Two hours from Grimholt, in a mountain pass. The barrow was deliberately collapsed on Ordovast's order. Nobody in Grimholt will say what got out before the collapse, or whether the collapse stopped it. The rubble shifts at night. The locals call it settling. The rubble has been settling for three years.

### Bleakwater Hollow (BAR_007)

Three hours from Bleakwater Landing, on the river bank where spring floods undercut the hillside two years ago and exposed a stone-lined chamber. The dead inside are waterlogged — bloated, slow, and relentless. They do not burn easily. River traders working the route between Bleakwater and Grimholt report seeing figures standing in the shallows after dark, waist-deep and motionless. The lower chambers flood with every rise in the water table. Drowning in the dark while the dead wade toward you is the specific nightmare this barrow offers.

### The War-Grave (BAR_008)

Eight hours from Grimholt along the mountain ridge. A mass burial from a battle that no saga records. The scale is wrong for a chieftain's grave — this is hundreds of warriors, friend and enemy thrown in together. Ordovast's scouts found weapon points pushing through the turf, as if the dead inside are pressing upward. The barrow is sealed. If the waking-barrows chain reaches this site, the sheer volume of dead could overrun Grimholt's defences in a single night.

## Deepholm Region

### Archive Barrow (BAR_009)

Two hours from Deepholm, in a mountain cavern that was expanded and lined with fitted stone centuries ago. This is Deepholm's barrow archive — not a warrior's grave but a scholar's and jarl's, buried with their records. Rune-carved tablets line the walls of the first hall like shelves. Deepholm's scholars enter periodically to consult the inscriptions but never pass the first hall. Beyond it waits a haugbui that has guarded the inner archive for an unknown span. The Grave Wardens' oldest texts reference records kept here — including the binding rite for the Draugr-Jarl beneath Frostfjord. Accessing the deeper chambers may be the only way to save Hrothgar's settlement.

### The Hollow Vein (BAR_010)

Six hours from Deepholm. Miners following an iron vein broke through a wall and found a burial chamber on the other side. The dead miners now walk the tunnels alongside the barrow's original occupants. The connecting shaft is narrow — no room for a shield wall, barely room to swing a hand-axe. The air in the deepest levels is toxic. Iron ore near the entrance has a blood-red tinge that the smiths say is wrong. Deepholm wants it cleared but the contract pay does not reflect the risk.

### Thornwall Passage (BAR_011)

Five hours from Thornwall, at the mountain's foot. A sealed tunnel mouth in the hillside, capped with a stone slab bearing rune-marks in a dialect nobody in the Rimevegr can read. The warding runes are still active — they resist tampering and have not degraded with time. Dalla described this barrow in exact detail during one of her unwanted visions, though she has never been within fifty miles of Thornwall. Whatever is inside is connected to her awakening. The rune dialect suggests construction before any known settlement in the region.

## Coastal Region

### Kolvik Barrow (BAR_012)

One hour from Kolvik, on a low coastal hill above the harbour. A minor barrow — small enough that most people forgot it was there until three cows were found dead around its capstone in Y312 spring, blood pooled in their ears, no wound visible. The ground near the stone was warm despite a cold morning. The farmer burned the carcasses rather than butcher them. Since then, corpse lights have been seen at dusk. The barrow is an early marker in the waking-barrows chain — small in itself, but its activation means the network is stirring.

### Stonebay Wreck-Grave (BAR_013)

Two hours from Stonebay Hamlet, built into the sea cliffs. A ship-burial — prow stones jut from the rock face like the bones of a longship frozen mid-crash. Storm surge breached the seaward wall last winter. Inside: a drowned captain, salt-preserved, leather-hard, still standing. Access is tide-dependent. At high water the entrance floods completely. At low tide you have perhaps four hours before the sea comes back. The approach is slick rock and narrow ledges with the ocean below. Two walking dead inside — manageable if you do not slip.

### Salt Cairn (BAR_014)

Six hours from Skaldhaven, in a sea cave at the base of the coastal cliffs. The cave mouth is sealed with fitted stone of a quality no living mason in the Rimevegr can match. Salt crystals grow across the seal and reform within hours of being broken. Fishermen avoid the cove — nets come up shredded, as if something in the water is pushing them away from the cave. Nobody knows what is inside. The craftsmanship of the seal, the regenerating salt, and the age of the stone all suggest pre-settlement construction.

## Moor Region

### Standing Stone Barrow (BAR_015)

One hour from Moor's End, marked by the standing stones that Elder Brosa forbids anyone from approaching after dark. The stones hum. Not a vibration from wind or settling earth — a sound with tone, almost like chanting heard from too far away to make out words. The Ghost Moor Crossing passes within sight of this barrow and travellers on that route report shadow-figures between the stones. A haugbui occupies the burial chamber beneath. Veil activity around the stones is unpredictable — seidr effects spike without warning. The open moor offers no cover and no retreat options if things go wrong.

### Feldwick Peat Grave (BAR_016)

Three hours from Feldwick, in the peat bogs that surround the settlement. Peat-cutters exposed a burial pit and the acid water preserved the bodies to the consistency of boiled leather. They still move. Black-skinned, leather-jointed, slow but terribly hard to damage — the peat has tanned them like hide. The bog will swallow anyone in armour who steps off the solid ground. Two dead, manageable from a distance, suicidal up close in terrain that fights you harder than the corpses do.

### Ghost Moor Cairns (BAR_017)

Ten hours from any shelter in any direction, in the middle of the Ghost Moor Crossing. A cluster of seven cairns, each sealed with a flat stone, arranged in a pattern that means nothing to anyone alive. The bypass route between Skaldhaven and Thornwall passes through them. Between the cairns, compasses spin and the stars look wrong — a Veil-field that distorts navigation. No saga mentions who built them or why seven were placed together. None of the seven has been opened. Seven sealed chambers. Any could be empty. Any could end a band.

### Helga's Watch (BAR_018)

Four hours from Thornwall, on a moorland hilltop with line of sight in every direction. Named after Helga Járnhand, a Thornwall shield-maiden buried three generations ago with her weapons, her arm-rings, and her oaths. Her descendants say the grave was disturbed from below — something in the hill pushed into Helga's chamber. Corpse lights circle the hilltop on clear nights. Five draugr occupy the barrow, including two of the armoured variety. The hilltop position means they see you coming long before you see them.

## Forest Region

### Ashmark Root-Grave (BAR_019)

Two hours from Ashmark, deep in the forest where the canopy blocks all light. Tree roots have grown through the barrow and some of those root systems are bone. The forest floor above the grave is dead — no moss, no undergrowth, bare black earth in a rough oval that the woodcutters refuse to cross. Inside, the root tangles create an unpredictable maze. Bone swarms nest in the knots where root and bone have fused. The darkness beyond the entrance is total. Seven or more draugr, disoriented by the root growth, wandering paths that change as the trees shift.

### Vargheim Pit (BAR_020)

Four hours from Vargheim, where the forest floor gave way into a burial chamber. A sinkhole three metres deep with worked stone at the bottom. Hunters found it when a dog fell in. The pit fills with groundwater every spring, submerging whatever is down there for most of the year. In a dry summer the water drops below the stone floor and the chamber is accessible — briefly, before autumn rains fill it again. Nobody has been down to look. The dog was pulled out alive but would not stop shaking for a week.

### The Widow's Barrow (BAR_021)

Five hours from Ashen Reach, in the pine forest where the tar works' smell does not reach. The entrance is carved with a woman's face — mouth open, screaming. The carving is maintained — someone re-cuts it when it weathers. The Pale Widow reportedly knows what is inside and will not speak of it. Pine tar from Ashen Reach's works seeps through the soil toward the barrow, not away from it, as if the ground slopes wrong. The barrow is sealed, warded, and forbidden. Whatever the Widow knows, it connects to her web of influence across the region.

## Mountain Region

### Raven's Perch Barrow (BAR_022)

One hour from Raven's Perch, on a mountain shelf overlooking the settlement. The largest active barrow in the Rimevegr. Home to the Barrow-King — Jarl Halvdan, dead three centuries and still sitting on his stone throne with a sword across his knees. The eyes burn pale blue. The mouth moves. He speaks in degraded Old Norse and expects obedience. Six to twelve draugr guard the outer halls, with barrow sentinels at choke points. The Barrow-King's throne room is the innermost chamber. The barrow has been sealed and reopened three times in three centuries. Each time, more dead walk the halls. Inside the throne room lies a map stone that reveals the locations of connected barrows across the entire region. Destroying the Barrow-King requires destroying the throne, the torc, and the barrow's keystone. An army-tier problem walked into through a mountainside door.

### Spine Ridge Ossuary (BAR_023)

Fourteen hours from Grimholt, above the treeline on the Spine. Built into the rock face — an ossuary where bones are stacked in alcoves from floor to ceiling. The mountain people say this is where dishonoured warriors go, those who died running or broke oaths. Wind through the chambers sounds like screaming. It may actually be screaming. A haugbui guards the deepest alcoves. The approach is half a day of exposed climbing above the treeline where altitude sickness and weather are as dangerous as anything dead. One path in. One path out. Sheer cliffs on three sides.

### High Pass Tomb (BAR_024)

Eight hours from Raven's Perch along the High Ridge bypass route. A stone slab blocks a cave mouth. Travellers on the Ridge bypass have reported the slab vibrating, and on the inner surface there are scratch marks — deep, parallel, made with fingers or claws by something that tried to get out. The camp site nearest the tomb is the only flat ground on that stretch of the Ridge. Smart bands sleep further along and accept the worse ground. The tomb is small, sealed, and minor — but those scratch marks are fresh.

## Ice Region

### Icebreak Complex (BAR_025)

One hour from Icebreak, beneath the ice sheet that covers the northern reaches. A network of interconnected burial chambers linked by ice tunnels, older than any human settlement in the Rimevegr. Rune-stones at each chamber entrance glow blue with a light that intensifies when living people approach. Hermit Ragnhild, the seidr practitioner who is Icebreak's only permanent resident, says the Veil is thinning permanently here. The rune-stones use a script that no living scholar can fully read. Twenty or more dead occupy the complex, including the most dangerous draugr variants and Veil-touched entities. The extreme cold kills faster than the dead do. Navigation is treacherous — ice tunnels branch, loop, and dead-end. Some of the oldest rune-stones may contain information about the First Barrow's anatomy.

### The First Barrow (BAR_026)

Six hours from Icebreak, through territory that resists travel. Paths curve back toward the hill. Streams flow toward it. Trees lean inward. The hill was hollow when the first settlers arrived. They buried their dead inside because it felt right. It always felt right. The barrow wanted the dead. It has been collecting them for longer than memory extends. The hill breathes — warm air out, cold air in. The entrance is a mouth. Inside, the corridors shift every ten minutes. Some dissolve organic matter. Rooms extract memories. The dead that dwell within function as an immune system, not guards — biological response, not malice. There is no fighting the First Barrow because you cannot fight terrain. The only path to destroying it lies in the heart chamber at its deepest point, where all the death-energy converges. A runestone at the original entrance — now overgrown, half-buried — maps the barrow's internal anatomy. The heart has thirty points of resilience and regenerates constantly. The entire Rimevegr's barrow network flows through this hill. Kill it, and every barrow in the region loses its connection to the death-energy web. Leave it, and it keeps growing.

## Connected Barrow Networks

The barrows of the Rimevegr are not isolated graves. A death-energy network links them — invisible, underground, flowing like water through root systems. The First Barrow (BAR_026) is the source. Every barrow that was dug into the Rimevegr's soil fed into this network, whether the diggers knew it or not.

The waking-barrows chain represents this network activating. As the First Barrow's heartbeat strengthens, the connected barrows stir. Kolvik Barrow (BAR_012) was the first sign — dead cattle. The Whispering Barrow (BAR_001) followed. Iron Barrow (BAR_005) opened. The pattern moves outward from the ice toward the coast, like a wave.

The Barrow-King at Raven's Perch (BAR_022) holds a map stone that shows the connections between barrows in the eastern region. The Archive Barrow at Deepholm (BAR_009) contains Grave Warden records that describe the network's nature. Ragnhild at Icebreak knows the First Barrow's location. These three sources — the map stone, the archive, and Ragnhild's knowledge — together provide the intelligence needed to understand what the Rimevegr is sitting on.

The sealed barrows are the most dangerous unknowns. Ghost Moor Cairns (BAR_017), the War-Grave (BAR_008), Salt Cairn (BAR_014), and Thornwall Passage (BAR_011) are all sealed, all ancient, and all connected to the network. When the network fully activates — the Barrow Tide (WBS_01) — every sealed barrow opens simultaneously.
