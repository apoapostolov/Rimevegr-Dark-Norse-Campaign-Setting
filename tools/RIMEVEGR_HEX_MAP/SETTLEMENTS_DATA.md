## Terrain Hazards by Type

### Coast (Fjords)

**Settlements:** Frostfjord Hollow, Ashen Reach, Kolvik, Stonebay Hamlet

- Tide windows (critical for fording)
- Cliff exposure and falls
- Sea-fog (navigation hazard)
- Cold water shock
- Storm surge in high seas
- Safe harbor: Only in settlements with ships

### Forest (Dense)

**Settlements:** Vargheim, Ashmark, Feldwick, Skaldhaven

- Low visibility (max 50 feet in heavy rime)
- Navigator errors (2+ hexes easily)
- Wolf pressure (1-2 packs per 10 days on route)
- Root hazards (twisted ankles, fallen timber)
- The Hush (eerie silence, fear response)
- Travel cost: +1 day per 2 hexes vs. open terrain

### Moorland

**Settlements:** Grimholt, Thornwall, Moor's End

- Exposure death (hypothermia risk in Long Dark)
- Sinkholes and bog pockets (navigation trap)
- Orientation failure (featureless terrain)
- Shelter scarcity (need palisade settlement or camp fully fortified)
- Wind constant (auditory fatigue)

### Mountains

**Settlements:** Raven's Perch

- Pass closure (winter = impassable)
- Scree falls (movement hazard)
- Altitude fatigue (doubled from extended ascent)
- Choke points (vulnerability to ambush)
- Thin air (exertion penalties at altitude)

### Bog/Marsh

**Settlements:** Deepholm, Bleakwater Landing, (parts of Thornwall region)

- Soft ground (wagon/large creature slow)
- Navigation failure (2-4 hexes possible)
- Drowning risk (water unexpectedly deep)
- Midges in summer (low-level harassment)
- Disease potential (wounds infected by bog water)

### Ice (Far North)

**Settlements:** Icebreak

- Whiteout and crevasse risk
- Extreme cold lethality (Long Dark exposure = death in 6-12 hours)
- Very low forage (hunting failure, food scarcity)
- Delayed rescue likelihood (if stranded, no aid for days)
- Veil activity (supernatural phenomena density increases)

## Seasonal Considerations

### Long Summer (60 days approx)

- All routes passable
- Coastal routes best (low water hazard)
- Forest routes dangerous (insect pressure, aggressive growth)
- Shield walls difficult (mud drag)

### Transition (1 week, dangerous)

- River crossings shift from ice-ruts to impassable flood
- Mountain passes destabilize (scree slide risk)
- Infrastructure failures (shelters collapse)
- Best to shelter in-settlement

### Long Dark (300 days, harsh)

- Mountain passes close (snow)
- Forest takes 50% longer (visibility, forage)
- Moorland survival margin thin (shelter must be confirmed)
- Ice routes become possible (frozen rivers = highway)
- Coast becomes dangerous (storm frequency)
- Every hex costs more energy/supplies

## Hex Coordinate Math (for GMs)

**Distance between two hexes:**

- Use cube coordinates: (q, r, s) where s = -q-r
- Distance = (abs(q1-q2) + abs(r1-r2) + abs(s1-s2)) / 2

**Example: Deepholm (6,7) to Grimholt (8,6)**

- Deepholm cube: (6, 7, -13)
- Grimholt cube: (8, 6, -14)
- Distance = (|6-8| + |7-6| + |-13-(-14)|) / 2 = (2 + 1 + 1) / 2 = 2 hexes

**Travel time formula:**

- Base: 6 miles per hex ≈ 1 day of regular travel
- Modify by terrain: Forest +50%, Moor +25%, Mountain +100%, Ice +100%
- Cold Dark penalty: +50% to all times
- Wounded/sick: +50% for each casualty affecting morale
