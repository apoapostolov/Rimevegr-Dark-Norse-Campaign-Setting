# Y313 Long Dark and Aftermath

## Purpose

This file is reserved for the later Y313 collapse phase after the main
Deepholm campaign.

Use it for the Long Dark convergence: famine, desertion, political
fracture, forced truces, dead patrols, widening barrow movement, and the
psychological and social damage that the war leaves behind.

  aftermath

## 1. Prompt 14 — the Y313 Long Dark horror escalation

Prompt 14 should make it impossible to pretend that the war and the dead
are separate problems. The people of the Rimevegr go into winter already
exhausted, and the supernatural pressure rises at exactly the point where
human systems are least capable of answering it.

### Long Dark doctrine

- dread should rise through repetition, silence, and confirmation rather
  than
through random spectacle
- every supernatural sign should land harder because food, warmth, and
  social
trust are already failing
- temporary truces and exhausted factions should feel plausible because
  neither
side can afford to ignore the dead anymore
- the dead should move from rumor to organized threat without losing the
  Norse
burial-dread tone

### Escalation streams

| Escalation stream       | What the people see                                                               | Structural effect                                                     |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| dead patrols and lights | lights on ridges, figures in formation, barrow mouths no longer staying shut      | the threat becomes territorial rather than local                      |
| silence and absence     | emptied roads, abandoned ward posts, vanished foragers, quiet halls               | fear starts changing daily behavior before open attack                |
| truce under pressure    | human enemies pause or narrow their fight because the deeper threat is undeniable | politics bends but does not become simple friendship                  |
| spiritual contamination | Dalla's condition worsens, old names return, and the land feels watched           | the Veil becomes an active condition rather than a background concept |

### Prompt 14 implementation rule

Each Long Dark chapter should do at least two things at once:

- worsen survival conditions
- confirm a supernatural pattern
- strain or re-route a human alliance
- expose who can still act clearly under dread

If a horror scene could be removed without altering logistics, morale, or
politics, it is not integrated enough.

## 2. Main Long Dark objects

### O313-H01 — Truce by Necessity, Not Trust

- **Year window:** Y313 first winter, days 220-260
- **Class:** political-horror convergence object
- **POV:** Voss, Petra, or a settlement witness
- **Canon anchors:** EVT_313_W02_077, EVT_313_W06_081, EVT_313_W12_087
- **Setup:** both major factions are too drained to keep clean pressure on
  each
other, and the first serious truce gestures happen under famine and dread
rather than goodwill
- **Decision pressure:** can the band use the truce to keep people alive
  without
mistaking pause for peace?
- **Competent options:** secure food corridors, verify terms with
  witnesses, keep
watches active, and assume opportunists will still exploit the lull
- **Desperate options:** disarm too much, trust old enemies too fast, or
  use the
truce to settle petty scores while worse things move outside the walls
- **Likely state touches:** truce_stability, winter_supply, faction_blame,
widow_power, local_trust
- **Routes:**
  - **O313-H02** — the truce exposes how little protection law now gives.
  - **O313-H03** — dead movement turns the pause from politics into crisis.
  - **O314-R01** — whatever survives this truce shapes the next year's
    order.
- **Mystery-box note:** answers why enemies pause, but not whether the
  pause is
enough to face what is actually coming

### O313-H02 — Roads with No Witnesses

- **Year window:** Y313 winter, days 224-275
- **Class:** disappearance and dread object
- **POV:** Petra, Gest, or a low-rank survivor witness
- **Canon anchors:** EVT_313_W04_079, EVT_313_W10_085, EVT_313_W14_089
- **Setup:** patrols vanish, green lights move in lines across the hills,
  and the
few survivors report not battle but organized absence, as if the land
itself is being emptied lane by lane
- **Decision pressure:** who still dares carry messages, grain, or wounded
  people
once the roads themselves begin feeling claimed?
- **Competent options:** travel in disciplined groups, mark return
  deadlines,
abandon dead routes quickly, and treat every unexplained silence as usable
data
- **Desperate options:** split up in storms, send unready boys alone, or
  assume a
familiar road is safe because it was safe in summer
- **Likely state touches:** road_access_state, patrol_fear,
  barrow_awareness,
civilian_panic, messenger_loss
- **Routes:**
  - **O313-H03** — the road dread hardens into confirmed dead formations.
  - **O313-H04** — communities begin changing law and ritual in response.
  - **O314-R02** — survivors carry these memories into the revelation year.
- **Mystery-box note:** answers that the roads are no longer neutral
  ground, but
not where the lines of dead are truly marching toward

### O313-H03 — The Dead Begin to Muster

- **Year window:** Y313 deep winter, days 260-325
- **Class:** direct horror-escalation object
- **POV:** Thorne, Dalla-adjacent witness, Petra, or Voss
- **Canon anchors:** EVT_313_W13_088, EVT_313_W19_094, EVT_313_W22_097
- **Setup:** by the late Long Dark, the dead are no longer isolated
  apparitions;
they are behaving like a gathering force, and the Wardens openly admit they
are losing the count
- **Decision pressure:** do the living reframe the crisis in time, or keep
  using
war habits that are now too small for the thing rising under the land?
- **Competent options:** consolidate the vulnerable, listen to Warden
  evidence,
protect interpreters like Dalla, and stop thinking in purely seasonal terms
- **Desperate options:** deny the scale, answer the dead like ordinary
  raiders,
or let faction prestige override common survival
- **Likely state touches:** veil_pressure, grave_warden_trust,
  regional_morale,
thorne_curse_stage, endgame_seed_flags
- **Routes:**
  - **O313-H04** — the living begin adapting law, ritual, and patrol
    behavior.
  - **O314-R01** — the next year opens under the shadow of a known deeper
    threat.
  - **O315-E01** — the endgame seeds become unavoidable.
- **Mystery-box note:** answers that the dead are organizing, but not yet
  what
buried will is ordering them

### O313-H04 — Ashes, Oaths, and the Last Human Order

- **Year window:** Y313 late Long Dark, days 300-360
- **Class:** end-of-year convergence object
- **POV:** Voss, Gest, Petra, or Dalla-facing witness
- **Canon anchors:** EVT_313_W15_090, EVT_313_W17_092, EVT_313_W21_096,
EVT_313_W22_097
- **Setup:** famine deaths mount, Ordovast rules by hanging, Petra and
  Thorne's
private debts sharpen, and the dead sing beyond the walls while the old
order tries to pretend it is still intact
- **Decision pressure:** what fragments of human structure are still worth
preserving before the year finally breaks?
- **Competent options:** protect the band's core trust, keep records,
  preserve
routes to allies, and decide which obligations will matter in the next year
- **Desperate options:** purge in fear, abandon the weak, squander the last
  food,
or make oath promises that the land can no longer support
- **Likely state touches:** band_cohesion, trust_survivors,
  famine_pressure,
political_legitimacy, revelation_readiness
- **Routes:**
  - **O314-R01** — carries directly into Y314 reconstruction and survivor
    triage.
  - **O314-R03** — ensures the revelation year inherits these year-end
    scars.
  - **Audit checkpoint** — gives the next consistency pass a clean
    late-Y313
proof point.
- **Mystery-box note:** answers what the Long Dark does to the living, but
  not
yet what final cost the truth beneath the barrows will demand
