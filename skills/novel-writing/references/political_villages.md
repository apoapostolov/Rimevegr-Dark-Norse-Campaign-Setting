# Political Scene Construction — Writing Rules

This reference is intentionally **craft-only**.

Canonical settlement and union lore has been moved to core docs:

- `12_POLITICAL_VILLAGES_AND_UNIONS.md`
- `11_VILLAGES_AND_SETTLEMENTS.md`

Mechanical handling remains in `20_SIMULATION_RULES.md` and
`scripts/village_politics.py`.

---

## Political Scene Rules

1. Every political scene must revolve around a concrete resource,
   obligation, or risk.
2. Dialogue should negotiate terms, not explain world history.
3. Escalation should move through behavior shifts (trade refusal,
   militia movement, oath hardening), not narration about stakes.
4. Keep authority dynamics visible via who speaks first, who closes,
   and who can refuse without immediate punishment.
5. End scenes on operational consequence (route, tribute, alliance,
   retaliation), not thematic commentary.

---

## Escalation Ladder (Craft Form)

Use this progression when structuring multi-scene political tension:

1. Constraint appears.
2. Terms are offered.
3. Terms are revised under pressure.
4. One side pays visible cost.
5. New equilibrium or open feud.

---

## Continuity Checks

- No settlement appears with contradictory strategic behavior in
  adjacent chapters without an explicit trigger.
- Resource scarcity must appear in logistics language, not abstract
  policy language.
- Political outcomes must propagate into contract availability,
  travel safety, and band morale pressure.

---

## Update Protocol

When world-state politics changes, update `12_POLITICAL_VILLAGES_AND_UNIONS.md`
first. This file should only change when writing-process rules change.
