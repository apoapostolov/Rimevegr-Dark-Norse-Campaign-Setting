# Rimevegr pantheon symbols and iconography

<!-- markdownlint-disable MD024 -->

This skill reference defines how the gods appear in prompt work for the Rimevegr.

The gods do not present themselves as clean divine portraits. They survive in the world through crude carvings, scratched shrine marks, soot-black icons, lintel cuts, burial tokens, law-stones, god-posts, whalebone etchings, and old signs half-worn by weather and hands.

## Core rendering law

Always prefer:

- rough wood carving
- stone scratches
- soot-black icons on beams
- bone or antler tokens
- knife-cut signs on doorposts
- grave goods, rune-stones, law circles, ferry posts, shrine corners

Never default to:

- glowing full-body god portraits
- polished divine avatars in the sky
- high-fantasy celestial beings
- clean modern symbol design
- ornate temple mural art with bright color

If a god appears in an image, it should usually be through the mark of belief, not through literal manifestation.

## Repeatability law for exact symbols

These symbols should read like stable folk-glyphs, not loose inspiration.

- Each god gets one primary canonical glyph that should repeat across carvings,
  tokens, lintels, and shrine marks.
- Lock the silhouette first, then the orientation, then the mandatory objects,
  then the number of major cuts or divisions.
- Change only material, weathering, soot, moss, frost, scale, and roughness.
  Do not change the core symbol design.
- Prefer centered, front-facing, icon-like carvings rather than narrative
  illustrations.
- If the model starts inventing extra knotwork, animals, crowns, or fantasy
  ornament, strip back to the primary glyph only.

## Exact key icon logic

Use these as the default locked symbols:

- Odynn: an upside-down one-eyed hanging figure beneath a forked branch, with a
  single rope loop and strong vertical symmetry
- Thurr: a blunt split-fist war sigil with four knuckle cuts and one central
  crack, or the same crack repeated in both hands of a blocky figure
- Fraeja: a gaunt tear-cut face with a spindle on one side and a child skull or
  bundle on the other
- Lopt: a face with a sewn horizontal mouth using three visible cross-stitches,
  plus one inverted warning mark underneath
- Hael: a seated figure cut straight down the middle, one half flesh and one
  half bone
- Tyvr: a rigid one-armed law figure with the missing hand given to a wolf jaw,
  never scales and never balanced symmetry
- Skathi: a faceless narrow huntress with a drawn bow and three ice-triangle
  cuts
- Bragi: a broken lyre with exactly three snapped strings and an open mouth
  notch above or within the frame
- Heimr: a threshold sign built from an open ring pierced by one vertical line,
  often flanked by two short gate-post cuts

## Material language of worship

Use these surfaces repeatedly:

- driftwood god-posts
- frost-cracked stone lintels
- smoke-black longhouse beams
- standing stones near moors and barrows
- grave markers and thing-stones
- bone charms and scratched antler tags
- ship ribs, ferry posts, door frames, meal tables, and burial lids

Use these atmospheric cues:

- old knife cuts filled with soot
- tallow sheen on carved recesses
- moss in stone grooves
- dried blood or ale stains from offerings
- frost gathering in the deeper cuts of the symbol

## The High Court

## Odynn the One-Hung

### Symbolic marks

- hanging figure beneath a dead tree limb
- one empty eye socket
- rope loop and single eye motif
- forked branch over a vertical hanging line

### Exact glyph lock

- primary glyph: an upside-down human form hanging from a forked branch
- mandatory elements: one rope loop, one hollow eye, arms hanging down, body on
  a single vertical axis
- allowed variation: the legs may be closed or slightly split, but the figure
  must remain inverted and centered
- never add ravens, spears, crowns, extra eyes, or standing poses to the core
  symbol

### Crude in-world depiction

Show Odynn as a rough man-shape upside down on an ash trunk, one eye cut deeper than the other, blackened resin or soot in the hollow. The carving should feel fearful and oath-heavy.

### Best placements

- oath stones
- contract stones
- captain's travel shrines
- gallows posts

### Prompt addon

> crude Odynn carving on weathered wood or stone, one empty eye and rope-loop motif, oath-heavy dread, old soot rubbed into the cuts

Avoid:

- wise wizard-god portraiture
- noble all-father grandeur

## Thurr the Crack-Handed

### Symbolic marks

- split fist
- broken-knuckle handprint
- blocky broad-shouldered war figure with cracked hands

### Exact glyph lock

- primary glyph: a front-facing fist or paired fists with four knuckle marks and
  one hard vertical split through the center
- mandatory elements: blunt geometry, cracked hands, no delicate fingers, no
  lightning bolts
- allowed variation: the fist may appear alone or attached to a broad torso, but
  the central break must remain the dominant shape
- never drift into a storm emblem, hammer emblem, or comic-book thunder sign

### Crude in-world depiction

A blocky broad-shouldered war sign, hacked fast into wood or stone by frightened
fighters before battle, with the crack in the fist carrying the whole symbol.

### Best placements

- shield backs
- gate posts
- training yards
- battle cairns

### Prompt addon

> rough Thurr war mark, split-knuckle hand motif, bruised and cracked battle symbol hacked into wood, blunt desperate strength

Avoid:

- thunderbolt superhero imagery
- polished storm-god glamour

## Fraeja the Grief-Bride

### Symbolic marks

- distaff and skull
- tear-grooves cut down a plain face
- barren-field spiral with one seed at the edge
- hair-lock burial token

### Exact glyph lock

- primary glyph: a long-faced woman marked by tear grooves, with a spindle on
  one side and a child skull or wrapped child-bundle on the other
- mandatory elements: downward face, visible tears, feminine stillness,
  asymmetry between spindle and child-loss token
- allowed variation: the child token may be a small skull or a swaddled bundle,
  but one of them must always be present
- never turn the symbol into a lush fertility emblem, flower crown, or lover
  goddess portrait

### Crude in-world depiction

A gaunt female figure with an emptied face, a spindle in one hand and a small skull or child-bundle in the other. The carving should feel intimate, private, and painfully local.

### Best placements

- women's shrine corners
- burial markers
- private boxes, bed frames, or cradle wood
- cook-fire and seidr spaces

### Prompt addon

> crude Fraeja grief icon scratched into wood, tear-worn face, spindle and skull motif, private mourning symbol with smoke and ash residue

Avoid:

- lush fertility goddess beauty
- jeweled goddess portraiture

## Lopt the Scar-Mouth

### Symbolic marks

- sewn mouth
- upside-down runestone sign
- crossed cuts over lips
- fire notch beside a face-sign

### Exact glyph lock

- primary glyph: a face-mask with one horizontal mouth line stitched shut by
  three cross-stitches
- mandatory elements: mouth seam, unsettling eyes, one inverted warning mark
  below or beside the face
- allowed variation: the eyes may be hollow or slit-like, but the mouth must be
  visibly sewn
- never render Lopt as a horned devil, clown, or ornate trickster crest

### Crude in-world depiction

A laughing-eyed face with the mouth crossed shut or stitched. It should look uncomfortable and half-defaced on purpose.

### Best placements

- hidden messages
- spy caches
- warning stones
- places where betrayal, arson, or truth-telling matter

### Prompt addon

> upside-down Lopt mark, face with sewn mouth and laughing eyes, rough warning symbol, truth and betrayal carved in hostile shorthand

Avoid:

- trickster-jester imagery
- theatrical devil coding

## Hael the Half-Rotted

### Symbolic marks

- half-face split between flesh and bone
- seated figure on a plain chair
- rib and spiral grave mark
- dividing line down a body form

### Exact glyph lock

- primary glyph: a seated body split by one straight vertical centerline
- mandatory elements: chair or stool base, one fleshed half, one skeletal half,
  no motion
- allowed variation: flesh may appear plain and bone may appear ribbed, but the
  seated stillness and half-and-half division must remain
- never render Hael as a floating queen, crowned skull, or battle-posed reaper

### Crude in-world depiction

A seated female form divided straight down the middle, one side smooth, one side cut skeletal with green-grey staining or lichen.

### Best placements

- barrow doors
- grave goods
- burial stones
- draugr-warning marks

### Prompt addon

> crude Hael icon on grave-stone, split living-and-bone body, seated death figure, moss and age in the cuts, cold indifferent funerary mood

Avoid:

- glamorous death goddess styling
- necromancer queen visuals

## The Low Court

## Tyvr the Stump-Armed

### Symbolic marks

- one-armed law figure
- hand in a wolf jaw
- scale replaced by silver rings or cut marks
- justice notch on thing-stones

### Exact glyph lock

- primary glyph: a rigid standing law figure with one arm intact and the other
  ending in or missing into a wolf jaw
- mandatory elements: asymmetry, upright posture, one-armed sacrifice, public
  legal severity
- allowed variation: the wolf may be a jaw-shape or bite-cut at the missing arm,
  but the figure must never regain balance or a second full arm
- never replace this with scales, books, crowns, or symmetrical judgment icons

### Crude in-world depiction

A standing one-armed figure with the missing hand implied by a wolf-mouth cutout or bite-mark. The carving should feel legal, public, and severe.

### Best placements

- thing circles
- law stones
- market posts
- weregild markers

### Prompt addon

> rough Tyvr law symbol cut into assembly stone, one-armed judge shape, wolf-bite sacrifice motif, public justice marker worn by weather and hands

Avoid:

- neat modern scales-of-justice logos
- Romanized law iconography

## Skathi the Bone-Walker

### Symbolic marks

- faceless huntress shape
- ice-shard triangles
- sinew bow
- liver-offering slab mark

### Exact glyph lock

- primary glyph: a narrow faceless huntress with a drawn bow and three small
  ice-triangle cuts
- mandatory elements: no face, lean body, bow in tension, winter-shard geometry
- allowed variation: the triangles may sit above, below, or behind the figure,
  but there should be three of them and the bow must remain drawn
- never turn Skathi into a detailed princess archer, antler queen, or animal
  totem collage

### Crude in-world depiction

A narrow female outline with no face, just a frost-shape head and a drawn bow. Keep the carving spare and wind-cut.

### Best placements

- hunting stones
- mountain cairns
- cold routes and ice shelters
- kill-offering slabs

### Prompt addon

> crude Skathi hunt symbol on cold stone, faceless bow-woman formed from frost-like cuts, severe winter survival icon

Avoid:

- glamorous snow queen imagery
- elegant fantasy archer goddess art

## Bragi the Unstrung

### Symbolic marks

- broken harp or lyre frame
- snapped line ends
- open mouth over broken strings
- memory-knot and recitation notch marks

### Exact glyph lock

- primary glyph: a broken lyre or harp frame with exactly three snapped strings
- mandatory elements: snapped ends, open mouth notch or recitation gap, memory
  rather than celebration
- allowed variation: the mouth notch may sit above the lyre or cut into the top
  of the frame, but the broken three-string structure must remain dominant
- never turn Bragi into a court minstrel, decorative music crest, or elegant
  intact instrument

### Crude in-world depiction

A simple man-shape holding a bent harp with broken lines instead of strings, more memorial than musical.

### Best placements

- skald benches
- archive posts
- memorial stones
- feast halls during recitation

### Prompt addon

> weathered Bragi carving with broken harp strings, memory symbol on a hall beam or saga-stone, austere and grief-heavy rather than celebratory

Avoid:

- bardic flamboyance
- pretty minstrel imagery

## Heimr the Gate-Blind

### Symbolic marks

- circle pierced by a vertical line
- white-eyed face at a gate
- horn held but not lifted
- threshold notch repeated on doorposts

### Exact glyph lock

- primary glyph: an open ring pierced by one vertical line, often set between
  two short gate-post cuts
- mandatory elements: threshold geometry, blind or empty eyes if figurative,
  horn lowered rather than raised
- allowed variation: the symbol may be purely abstract or reduced to a gate face,
  but the ring-and-line threshold structure must remain
- never turn Heimr into a triumphant herald, sunburst, or angelic gate symbol

### Crude in-world depiction

A standing watchman with blank eyes and one hand on an unused horn. Usually very simple, almost abstract.

### Best placements

- doorposts
- palisade gates
- watchtowers
- ferries and bridges

### Prompt addon

> crude Heimr threshold mark on a gate or doorpost, vertical line through circle, blind-eyed watchman symbol, old warning sign that comforts more than protects

Avoid:

- shining guardian angel presentation
- heroic horn-blower spectacle

## Fast repeatable prompt formulas

Use these when the image model needs a locked icon instead of loose inspiration.

- Odynn: rough folk-glyph of an upside-down one-eyed hanged man beneath a forked branch, one rope loop only, centered vertical carving
- Thurr: blunt split-fist war sigil with four knuckle cuts and one deep central crack, hacked into wood or shield-back
- Fraeja: gaunt tear-cut face with a spindle on one side and a child skull or bundle on the other, intimate grief symbol
- Lopt: mask-face with a sewn horizontal mouth using three cross-stitches and one inverted warning mark below
- Hael: seated half-flesh half-bone figure divided by one straight centerline, funerary stillness
- Tyvr: rigid one-armed law figure with the missing hand lost to a wolf jaw, severe public marker
- Skathi: faceless narrow huntress with a drawn bow and three ice-triangle cuts, wind-cut winter icon
- Bragi: broken lyre with exactly three snapped strings and an open recitation notch, memory-heavy beam carving
- Heimr: threshold sigil of an open ring pierced by one vertical line between two short gate-post cuts, blind watchman logic

## How to use divine iconography in scene prompts

### Domestic scenes

Use one small mark only:

- a soot-black Fraeja scratch near a bed
- a Heimr sign on a doorpost
- a Tyvr notch on a household chest

### Hall scenes

Use one public symbol and one private trace:

- Odynn contract mark on a post
- Bragi carving on the skald bench
- Thurr shield mark near the entry

### Barrow and grave scenes

Use Hael first, then optionally Odynn or Fraeja.

### Law and trade scenes

Use Tyvr, Odynn, or Heimr marks on stones, posts, or tally spaces.

### Veil and dread scenes

Keep symbols weathered and half-obscured. Faith in the Rimevegr should feel persistent, tired, and culturally unavoidable.

## Final anti-drift rules

- gods are culturally everywhere but visually indirect
- the carving should feel made by frightened or dutiful human hands
- worship is poor, local, and weather-beaten
- use one primary glyph per mark unless the scene explicitly calls for a shared shrine wall
- if a divine symbol becomes the clean centerpiece of the image, it is probably too polished
